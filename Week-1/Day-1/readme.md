 1. What is CRM?
CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system used by companies to manage interactions with customers.
It helps store customer data in one place
Tracks communication (calls, emails, meetings)
Improves customer relationships
Helps businesses increase sales
 Example:
A company stores all customer details like name, phone number, and purchase history in CRM.

 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce
Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform.
 Reasons companies use Salesforce:
Centralized customer data
Automation of business processes
Sales tracking and forecasting
Better customer support
Real-time analytics and reports
 Example:
Sales teams can track deals and know which customers are ready to buy.

 3. Core Salesforce Concepts
 Account
Represents a company or organization
Stores company-level information
 Example:
Infosys, Amazon, TCS
👤 Contact
Represents a person related to an account
Stores personal details like name, email, phone
 Example:
Employee or customer working in a company
Opportunity
Represents a sales deal
Tracks potential revenue and deal stage
 Example:
Selling a software package worth ₹1,00,000

5. Business Flow (Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer)
🔄 Flow Explanation:
Lead
A potential customer (not yet confirmed)
Example: Someone fills a website form
Contact
Qualified lead converted into a real person
Stored under an account
Opportunity
A deal is created when there is a chance of sale
Customer
When the deal is won, the person becomes a customer
 Simple Flow:
Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

6. Real-World Mapping (Example: College Admission System)
Concept	Example
Account	College/University
